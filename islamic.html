Islamic Era

With the arrival of Islam, Egypt entered a golden age of art, science, and architecture. This era transformed Cairo into a city of a thousand minarets, boasting breathtaking mosques, madrasas, and fortified citadels. The intricate geometric patterns, stunning calligraphy, and social hubs like Khan el-Khalili reflect a vibrant civilization that bridged the East and the West.

The Mosque of Muhammad Ali

An architectural masterpiece located inside the Citadel of Saladin, famous for its twin minarets and its ornate interior covered in Alabaster stone, making it one of the most visible and iconic landmarks in Cairo.

Al-Azhar Mosque

One of the most important architectural masterpieces and a world-renowned center for Islamic learning.

Ibn Tulun Mosque

The oldest mosque in Cairo that survives in its original form, famous for its unique spiral minaret and vast open courtyard.

Bab Zuweila

One of the remaining gates in the walls of the Old City of Cairo, representing a masterpiece of Mamluk military architecture.
