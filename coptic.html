Coptic Era

The Coptic Era is a profound chapter of Egypt's spiritual history, marking the spread of Christianity across the Nile. This period introduced unique artistic traditions, such as intricate textiles and iconic frescoes. The monasteries established during this time, often in remote desert locations, became sanctuaries of knowledge and faith, preserving the Coptic language and heritage for generations.

The Hanging Church

One of the oldest churches in Egypt, named for its location above a gatehouse of the Babylon Fortress

Monastery of Saint Anthony

The oldest Christian monastery in the world, located in a scenic oasis in the Eastern Desert.

Saint Catherine's Monastery

Located at the foot of Mount Sinai, it is one of the oldest working Christian monasteries in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage site.

The Coptic Museum

Home to the world's largest collection of Coptic art, bridging the gap between the Ancient Egyptian, Roman, and Islamic periods.
