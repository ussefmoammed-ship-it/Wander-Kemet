Choose Your Destination

Cairo

Sprawling, noisy, electric. Medieval mosques, ancient museums, and the pyramids on the city edge.

Alexandria

Egypt's Mediterranean soul — seafront promenades, Greco-Roman ruins, and excellent fresh seafood.

Luxor

The world's greatest open-air museum. Karnak, the Valley of the Kings, temples that still feel alive.

Aswan

Quieter than Luxor, just as beautiful. Nubian villages, felucca rides, and Abu Simbel nearby.

Giza

The last standing Wonder of the Ancient World. Nothing else comes close to seeing them in person.

Fayoum

Desert waterfalls, fossils, and a saltwater lake. Egypt's most underrated weekend escape.

Dahab

Sinai's relaxed dive town. Backpackers arrive for three days and end up staying for a month.

Hurghada

Popular Red Sea resort city. Warm water year-round, accessible reefs, and plenty of options for all budgets.

Siwa

Remote Berber oasis near Libya. Salt lakes, ancient oracle ruins, and some of the best stargazing in Africa.

Marsa Alam

Wild and uncrowded. Dugongs, shark reefs, and a coastline that hasn't been overdeveloped yet.

Sharm El Sheikh

Clear bays, vibrant coral, and resort infrastructure. A well-worn classic for a reason.

Minya

Cliff tombs, abandoned pharaonic cities, Coptic monasteries — and almost no other tourists around.
