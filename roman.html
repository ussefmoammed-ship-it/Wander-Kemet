Roman Era

Following the death of Cleopatra, Egypt became a vital province of the Roman Empire. This era witnessed a fascinating cultural fusion, where Roman administrative efficiency met Egyptian traditions. Cities like Alexandria flourished as centers of science and philosophy, leaving behind impressive theaters, catacombs, and temples that blend classical Greco-Roman styles with ancient Egyptian motifs.

Pompey's Pillar

A massive triumphal column in Alexandria, one of the best-known ancient monuments still standing today

Kom El Shoqafa Catacombs

A historical archaeological site considered one of the Seven Wonders of the Middle Ages.

The Roman Theater (Alexandria)

A unique semi-circular theater discovered in the heart of Alexandria, showcasing the grandeur of Roman entertainment and public life.

Temple of Dendera

One of the best-preserved temple complexes in Egypt, known for its stunning Roman-style ceiling paintings and astronomical charts.
